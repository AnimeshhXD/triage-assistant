CARDIAC EMERGENCY PROTOCOL

Indications:
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath
- Sweating or diaphoresis
- Nausea, vomiting

Immediate actions:
- Place patient on continuous cardiac monitor.
- Obtain 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of arrival.
- Establish IV access and obtain blood work including cardiac enzymes.
- Administer aspirin 160-325 mg chewed unless contraindicated.

Red flags:
- Hypotension or signs of shock.
- New ST-segment elevation on ECG.
- Severe shortness of breath or pulmonary edema.

Ongoing management:
- Consider nitroglycerin if blood pressure allows.
- Prepare for possible percutaneous coronary intervention.
