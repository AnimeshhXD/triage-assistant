TRAUMA AND INTERNAL BLEEDING PROTOCOL

Indications:
- Major trauma, falls, or motor vehicle collisions.
- Visible external bleeding.
- Abdominal pain after blunt trauma.

Immediate actions:
- Follow ABCs: airway, breathing, circulation.
- Apply direct pressure to external bleeding.
- Establish two large-bore IV lines and begin fluid resuscitation.
- Obtain focused assessment with sonography for trauma (FAST) when available.

Red flags:
- Hypotension or tachycardia.
- Altered mental status.
- Abdominal tenderness or distension.

Ongoing management:
- Prepare for emergent imaging and surgical consultation if internal bleeding suspected.
